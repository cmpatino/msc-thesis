The 0.6B benefits from increasing K for the smallest teacher, but its performance degrades when using more logprobs from the 235B teacher. \section{Catastrophic Forgetting} \label{sec: exps_forgetting}

\begin{figure}
    \centering
    \includegraphics[width=0.9\linewidth]{figures/teacher_scaling_gpqa_top1.png}
    \caption{Scores for GPQA for all students while training targeting math reasoning skills. The plots show how the 0.6B student suffers from catastrophic forgetting for the three methods, but also how SFT is the method that degrades performance the most. For the 4B student, all methods preserve the GPQA performance and even marginally improve it. These results show how the main driver in catastrophic forgetting is model capacity, and that on-policy methods are better to mitigate performance degradation.}
    \label{fig: gpqa_forgetting}
\end{figure}
