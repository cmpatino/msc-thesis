    \caption{Effect of increasing the top-k log probabilities. The 0.6B benefits from increasing K for the smallest teacher, but its performance degrades when using more logprobs from the 235B teacher. These results suggest that the 0.6B student cannot capture the increasing complexity of the large teacher but benefits from more details from a teacher within its reach. For the 4B student, scaling K does not have a positive impact since its performance remains constant or decreases for all teachers.}
